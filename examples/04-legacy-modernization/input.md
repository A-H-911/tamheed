# Project Brief — claims-portal-modernization (input as provided)

We need help figuring out how to modernize our claims portal. It's the web application our claims adjusters and policyholders use to file insurance claims and check their status. The thing was built about 12 years ago and it has become a real anchor around our necks.

The core problem is that it's one big monolithic application. The user interface, the business rules for claims, and all the database access are tangled together in the same codebase — you can't touch one part without worrying about breaking three others. There are essentially no automated tests, so every release is a white-knuckle event where QA spends a week manually clicking through screens. On top of that it runs on an aging runtime that our platform team keeps warning us is approaching end-of-support, and finding people who want to work in it is getting harder every year.

What we want is to modernize incrementally. We have heard the horror stories about big-bang rewrites and we are not interested in stopping the world for two years to rebuild everything and then flip a switch. Our preference is a strangler-fig style approach: stand up something in front of the old system and gradually move slices of functionality over to new services behind it, one piece at a time, while the old system keeps running. The portal cannot go dark — adjusters work claims during business hours and policyholders check status at all hours, so this has to stay live throughout.

A few things are non-negotiable. We must not lose any claim data — ever. We are a regulated insurer and we have retention obligations on claims and on the audit trail of who did what to a claim and when. That audit history has to stay complete and intact through whatever we do. We also have two integrations that absolutely cannot break: we read policy details from our policy administration system, and we hand off approved payouts to a payments gateway. Both have to keep working the entire time.

Things we would like, but that are softer: the adjuster screens have gotten slow and we would love for pages to load faster. And honestly the bigger prize is being able to ship features again at a reasonable pace instead of being terrified of the codebase.

We have not worked out the details and we are hoping you can help us think through them. Open questions on our side: which slice of functionality should we move first? What should the new stuff run on — we do not have a strong opinion yet and want a recommendation rather than us guessing. How do we handle cutover and rollback if a migrated slice misbehaves? And is there any downtime window we can live with, or does it truly have to be zero? We would rather you ask us the sharp questions than assume.
